Client Dependencies Are the Critical Path

This plan assumes Expedia provides: (1) Marketo data exports by Day 3, (2) Brand assets and email content direction by Day 7, (3) UAT sign-off within 48 hours of each gate, (4) Ad platform credentials for MCI connector setup by Day 10, (5) Campaign approval within 24 hours. Delays in any of these push the timeline. Two FDEs can work in parallel, but they cannot approve content or provide business context.

Campaign Dwell Time — Why 7 Days Minimum Days 17–28

Campaigns need time to generate meaningful data. You cannot measure ROI on Day 1 of a send. This plan builds in two 7-day campaign windows so that by Day 28, there is real engagement data (opens, clicks, conversions) to report on.

Days 1–3 Post-Send

Bulk of email opens/clicks arrive. Deliverability issues surface. Initial engagement rates visible.

Days 3–5 Post-Send

Click-through conversions start appearing. Ad retargeting audiences populate. SMS/WhatsApp delivery completes.

Days 5–7 Post-Send

Post-click attribution windows close (7-day). Conversion data complete enough for ROAS calculations. Enough data for credible ROI report.

Risk Register

What Could Slow This Down

HIGH Client Data Delivery Delays

If Marketo data exports arrive after Day 5, the entire Data Cloud track shifts right, which cascades into MCA (no segments without data) and MCI (no ROI without campaigns).

Mitigation: Pre-engagement data readiness checklist. Export templates provided in Week -1.

HIGH UAT Review Cycles > 48 Hours

If client stakeholders take a week to review emails or dashboards instead of 48 hours, Campaign 1 launch slips from Day 17 to Day 22+, compressing or eliminating the data collection window.

Mitigation: Dedicated client reviewer assigned pre-engagement. Daily 15-min sync during UAT gates.

MEDIUM Identity Resolution Quality

If the 4 Marketo instances have inconsistent email formats or heavy duplication, identity resolution match rates may be low, requiring ruleset tuning and re-runs.

Mitigation: FDE 1 runs sample identity resolution on Day 5 with a small dataset. Full run only after validation.

MEDIUM MCI Licensing Availability

MCI requires a separate license. If not provisioned on the org before Day 10, the entire MCI workstream is blocked. This must be confirmed during pre-engagement.

Mitigation: Confirm MCI license active during deal close. Fallback: MeshMesh generates ROI reports directly from Data Cloud queries (no MCI dependency).

LOW Campaign Volume Too Low for ROI

If POC email lists are small (<5K contacts), engagement data may be statistically insignificant. A/B test results may not be meaningful.

Mitigation: Target minimum 10K contacts per campaign. Supplement with historical ad data for ROI baselines.

LOW Calculated Insight SQL Limitations

Data Cloud CIs don't support DATEDIFF, CURRENT_DATE, or CAST. Rolling time windows require numeric date workarounds. Complex attribution models may hit SQL limits.

Mitigation: MeshMesh uses known workaround patterns. Core ROAS/CPA metrics are fully achievable within SQL constraints.

Steelman Assessment

Can This Actually Be Done in 30 Days?

An honest assessment accounting for human bottlenecks, campaign physics, and the reality that software doesn't run on its own.

The Math: Where Time Actually Goes

~55%
MeshMesh Automation

Data ingestion, DMO creation, identity resolution, segment building, CMS content creation, flow construction, Calculated Insights, activation targets, report generation. All programmatic. Two FDEs working in parallel means these tasks compress to days, not weeks.

~25%
Human Dependencies

Client data exports (Day 2-3), brand assets (Day 3-6), ad platform credentials (Day 9-11), campaign content approval (Day 15-17), dashboard UAT (Day 23-25), final presentation (Day 29-30). This is the critical path. FDEs are blocked during these windows.

~20%
Campaign Physics

Campaigns need 7 days to generate meaningful data. Emails need time for opens and clicks. Ad retargeting audiences need time to populate. Conversion attribution needs 7-day windows to close. You cannot compress physics — this time is non-negotiable.

Verdict: Yes, With Conditions

✓
Two FDEs make it feasible. FDE 1 (Data + Intelligence) and FDE 2 (Marketing Execution) work in parallel. While FDE 1 builds the data foundation in Week 1, FDE 2 prepares consent, content templates, and flow structures. No wasted time.
✓
MeshMesh compresses the build work dramatically. What takes an SI 2-3 weeks (data model, segments, flows, content), MeshMesh executes in 2-3 days per workstream. The automation is real — 95% of Data Cloud and MCA operations are programmatic.
⚠
Client responsiveness is the #1 risk. If any UAT gate takes more than 48 hours, the 30-day timeline is in jeopardy. A dedicated Expedia reviewer with 4-6 hrs/week availability is a hard requirement, not a nice-to-have.
⚠
ROI data will be directional, not definitive. With 7-10 days of campaign data, you get open rates, click rates, initial conversions, and early ROAS signals. You won't have full 28-day attribution. Frame the Day 30 presentation as "early indicators" with a clear path to full measurement at Day 60.
✓
UI-only tasks are manageable. TotalConnect pipeline creation, ad platform OAuth, and some MCI feature configuration require UI interaction. These are 2-4 hour tasks total — FDEs handle them alongside automated work. Not a blocker.
✓
Pre-engagement prep eliminates Week 1 waste. If Expedia pre-stages Marketo exports, brand assets, and ad platform access during deal close, Week 1 is pure build — no waiting. This is the single biggest accelerator.
